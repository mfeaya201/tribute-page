Seth MacFarlane: Career and Achievements

  • 1973: Born on October 26 in Kent, Connecticut.
  • 1982: Created a weekly comic strip, “Walter Crouton,” at age 9.
  • 1995: Graduated from RISD and joined Hanna-Barbera as an animator and writer.
  • 1999: Created *Family Guy* and became the youngest showrunner in TV history at age 25. Voices Peter, Stewie, and Brian Griffin.
  • 2005: Co-created *American Dad!*.
  • 2008: Created *Seth MacFarlane's Cavalcade of Cartoon Comedy*.
  • 2009: Co-created *The Cleveland Show*. Won a Webby Award for Film & Video Person of the Year.
  • 2012: Released *Ted*, which he wrote, directed, and starred in.
  • 2013: Hosted the Academy Awards and earned a nomination for Best Original Song for “Everybody Needs a Best Friend” from *Ted*.
  • 2014: Released *A Million Ways to Die in the West*.
  • 2015: Released *Ted 2*.
  • 2017: Created and starred in *The Orville*.
  • 2019: Received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 2020: Inducted into the Television Hall of Fame.
  • Music Career: Released eight studio albums in Sinatra’s style and performed at Carnegie Hall and Royal Albert Hall.
  • Cosmos: Executive producer of *Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey*.
  • Awards: *Family Guy* won multiple awards, including five Primetime Emmys.
